About Me

I didn’t plan to become a tutor — I became the tutor I once needed.

When I was a student, I struggled with direction. I was dealing with undiagnosed Asperger’s, battling depression, and tying my self-worth to exam results that didn’t reflect my potential. It wasn’t until Year 13 that I began to understand revision — and by then, it felt too late.

That experience shaped everything I do as a tutor today.

I now teach GCSE and A-Level students in Maths, Chemistry, Physics, and Biology. I support aspiring medics, resitters, and those who just need someone to believe they can do it. I break problems down, ask thoughtful questions, and use humour, mnemonics, and clarity to make learning click.

I also create my own blog, exam-style questions, and study packs — especially focused on the maths inside science. My mission is to help students not just pass, but believe in themselves. And to prove that it’s never too late to turn things around.
